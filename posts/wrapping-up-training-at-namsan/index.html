Wrapping Up Training at Namsan

May 16, 2021 · 3 min read

I was far from alone in terms of people who’s lives had been radically changed by covid. As a relatively neurotic person with slight ADHD I relied on the constant exercise sports provided to help calm my mind, allowing me to focus more in the classroom. Without this mental tempering my anxiety skyrocketed, my stress eating increased, and I felt like a shell of myself mentally and physically.

after going almost 6 months without stepping on the mats and then two weeks without being able to walk more than 3 meters in a straight line, I had never been so out of shape as an adult.

I had almost forgotten how much I needed physical exercise and slowly made my way back to training. A lesson or two a week but it wasn’t until I began teaching that I was able to rediscover my passion for grappling.

Coach Dave Doherty was more than welcoming and after rolling with me, a random blue belt, and getting heel hooked 3 times in the span of 5 minutes I was worried I would be met with anger as some “classical” BJJ gyms have a more machismo approach to the belt hierarchy.

Luckily Dave is a true martial artist and was happy that someone with leg lock experience had come to his gym. After a few weeks of training we decided that I would teach leg lock classes every friday in return for a free membership.

My first class was very jarring as some of the higher belts brought their partners most of whom were white belts with months of experience. One such spouse insisted that she must be too flexible or my heel hook too week after asking me to demonstrate the outside heel hook on her. Seconds later after slowly increasing tension on the joint her ankle cracked like a knuckle and my heart skipped a beat. I thought I had broken someone’s foot during my first class as teacher. Luckily she was fine and after that it was blue belts and above.

I had to relearn so much, turning to the 150 pages of picture supplemented notes taken during my time under John Danaher. To teach really is to learn for a second time. Sometimes I was rusty and felt like a horrible teacher but my students were very patient and after two months I started to see major improvements in their abilities and my capacity as a teacher.

It was a double edged sword on one hand my students were escaping my heel hook attempts, re-attacking, and showing the beginnings of a leg lock game; but on the other hand I no longer had a wild card that I could pull to get a quick sub.

Through my re-introduction into BJJ I felt myself slowly build back up and start to find solid ground once again. I am extremely grateful to Everyone at Namsan BJJ and especially to Dave for being so open to a random guy off the street who said they knew a thing about leg locks.
