Grappling

Wrestling

  • I began wrestling at 14 and decided it would be my only sport the last two years of high school (I was previously running track and playing football).
  • At Stuyvesant we didn’t have many people so I trained at the New York Athletic Club as well as at Beat the Streets big apple games summer camp.
  • I did alright my senior year and was one match away from going to the state tournament.
  • I wrestled a year on the Varsity team at CWRU finishing with a 6-10 record before focusing on BJJ full time.

Russian Sambo

  • to supplement my wrestling I started Russian jacket wrestling (Sambo) at New York Combat Sambo where I consistently trained for a year and a half.
  • The coaching was great and the classes were small, I developed a decent gi / jacket take-down game there and began learning leg locks.

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu

  • at 17 I began BJJ at Renzo Gracie Academy, as soon as I was able (three stripe white belt) I trained under John Danaher. During my gap year when I wasn’t working I was training and got my blue belt (not from John Danaher). I continued my training over the summer before going to college and wrestling for a year.
  • Started the CWRU grappling club which has yet to meet up due to Covid.
  • While in Korea training and teaching leg locks at Namsan JiuJitsu.
